Muscles & Movements:
Elbow, Wrist and Hand
1

After completing this module, you will be able to:
Name the attachments and function of muscles controlling the elbow, proximal radioulnar joint, wrist and fingers
Name the muscles of the anatomical snuffbox
Describe the relationship between joint movements, muscle shortening and muscle function for muscles at the elbow, radioulnar joint, wrist and fingers
Identify muscles in cross-sections of the upper limb

There are many motions of the upper limb so there are many muscles needed to control them
Flexors and extensors are needed at the elbow, wrist and fingers.

Supinators and pronators are needed at the proximal radioulnar joint.

Abductors and adductors are needed at the fingers, and ulnar and radial deviators are needed at the wrist.
